01 · Vision

A German Christmas Village inside the village Proposed

A holiday-themed pop-up village built around our existing buildings in the center of Marlborough, NH — tents, lights, music, food, and craft vendors transforming the town core into a walkable Weihnachtsmarkt experience for the Monadnock Region, with a community canned food drive benefiting the Marlborough Food Pantry.

Village within a village

The municipal lot, the Church, the Main House, and Stark Level Solutions form a connected festival campus inside the real town — not a fairground bolted onto it.

Pop-up format

Two weekends, Friday–Sunday. Build, run, strike. Start-small option on the table per Lindsay: prove the details, then expand.

Plug-and-play framework

The site plan, staffing model, and perimeter design are reusable: summer music festival, theatrical events, fall Halloween fling, community events.

Regional draw

An attention-catching anchor event for Marlborough, Keene, and the wider Monadnock Region, capable of accommodating thousands of visitors.

Operating structure

The overall event is a collaboration across The Stark Production Group businesses, The Marlborough House (special events entity), Stark Level Solutions, and SPG. On-site liquor service is provided under The Marlborough House's existing liquor license.

Community impact

Canned food drive for the Marlborough Food Pantry. Collection points throughout the village (tents, Church, Main House, Stark Level HQ, entrances). Attendees encouraged to bring non-perishables. Direct, high-impact local giving — a signature element of the municipal activation model.

04 · Venues

Venue programming Proposed

Main Hall / Church — “Hofbräu House”

Winter beer garden with a holiday selection of beverages (glühwein, German beer) and themed food offerings. Indoor vendor spaces possible at ~10'×10' equivalents in the chapel. Liquor service operated under The Marlborough House existing liquor license (secured via the special events business).

Main House — Christmas Fantasy

Christmas through and through, room by room: arts & crafts, ornament makers, themed installations per room as discussed.

Stark Level Solutions — HQ

Vendor check-in and pre-set-up, green room for performers and workers, production base, and restrooms.

Community House & back lane

Candidate areas for additional vendors and activities along the lane joining the municipal lot and Marlborough House.

Restrooms

Bathrooms exist at all our locations, but at this event scale a set of restroom trailers is likely necessary. Goal: avoid port-a-potties. In discussion

Entertainment

Per the Amherst blueprint: German and Christmas music, Santa (and Krampus) photo ops, festive light displays. Programming TBD.

Community Food Drive — Marlborough Food Pantry

Collection stations at vendor tents, Church/Hofbräu House, Main House, Stark Level HQ, and entrances. Attendees invited to bring canned goods and non-perishables. All donations delivered to the Marlborough Food Pantry. Volunteer support for sorting and transport. A simple, visible community service component that builds broad local support.

05 · Vendors

Vendor village — Municipal Lot Proposed

  • Capacity: at least 75 vendors in the standard tent format; ~100 with surrounding areas
  • Outdoor footprint: ~13'×13' per vendor (10'×10' tents plus buffer)
  • Indoor: chapel spaces at roughly 10'×10' equivalents
  • Atmosphere: themed lighting throughout, holiday signage and wayfinding pointing in all directions
  • Overflow: Community House grounds, back lane, and the serpentine driveway behind the house

06 · Parking

Satellite parking & Christmas Cabooses Proposed

  • Marlborough Elementary School — enormous lot, ~0.5 mile from town center
  • McNabb Slabs end — large parking area
  • Behind former Mother's (current studio location) — large area directly behind
  • General store lot — across from Town Hall and police HQ, walking distance
  • Dave's Junkyard — tremendous amount of space (large area highlighted in the Google Earth reference imagery). As we project maximum possibilities for the event (thousands of visitors), a properly planned partnership — potentially including a fee or revenue share to the owners — could provide a major additional parking resource. We can always scale down from the maximum.
  • “Christmas Cabooses” — stretch golf carts or other thematically decorated conveyances shuttling visitors to and from the central event

07 · Safety

Traffic, safety & perimeter In discussion

Route 101 management — the biggest issue

Designated crossing points staffed by traffic guards, plus appointees managing pedestrian flow. Full discussion required with the town and local police.

Themed perimeter “prop fence”

Perimeter barrier in keeping with the Christmas theme, with entrances aligned to existing driveways. Ties in with neighbors: the Getty, the Fire Department, and the assisted living homes forming one side of the village.

Neighbor partnership

Assisted living residents offered free VIP passes for all festivities — an easy sell that supports the security perimeter. Our properties plus Main Crust form the other perimeter; relationships there are strong.

Back-of-envelope figures from the working thread; 2 weekends (6 days) at 5,000 adults/day × $10 = ~$300k gross gate. Of this, 5% (~$15k total) is proposed to go to the Town of Marlborough for a "special projects" fund. Net gate ~$285k + ~$22.5k vendors = roughly $307.5k+ before premium booths and concessions. This proposal aligns with Amherst's pricing ($10/adult, kids free).

10 · Approvals

Approvals & stakeholders In discussion

Town of Marlborough

Full discussion required on setup, logistics, parking, and overall event format. Lindsay's documented success in Amherst (incl. Board of Selectmen approvals) is the proof case. As a direct community benefit, we propose 5% of all gate receipts (~$15,000 total over two weekends) go to the Town for a "special projects" fund.

Liquor licensing for on-site service is already secured through The Marlborough House (the special events entity). The overall event brings together The Stark Production Group businesses, The Marlborough House, Stark Level Solutions, and SPG.

Local police

Route 101 traffic management, crossing points, and crowd flow must be designed jointly with police. Amherst precedent: department heads engaged early, safety as top priority.

Key local supporters

Strategy: secure the support of key individuals first, then bring the package to the town. Town businesses already approached — all in favor.

Neighbors

The Getty, Fire Department, assisted living homes (VIP passes), and Main Crust. Relationships are friendly; formal buy-in to be confirmed.

Community House

Expected to get on board with using the spaces around the municipal lot. To be confirmed.

Marlborough Food Pantry

Primary beneficiary of the on-site canned food drive component. A direct, in-kind community benefit that strengthens town and neighbor support and positions the event as a net positive activator for the whole village.

School / lot owners

Permissions for satellite parking at the elementary school, McNabb Slabs area, the studio lot, and the general store lot.

11 · Open Questions

Open questions In discussion

  • Dates — which two weekends? (Lindsay's direct ask; Friday–Sunday format anticipated)
  • Vendor count — confirm capacity via measured ground plan of the municipal lot, driveway, and chapel
  • Restrooms — restroom trailers: how many, where staged, cost; avoid port-a-potties if possible
  • Ticketing — $10/adult (kids free) vs. other structures; hourly caps like Amherst's 1,000/hour model; timed entry
  • Scale of year one — Lindsay favors starting small and expanding; JJ's full campus vision is the target state. Phasing to agree
  • Caboose logistics — vehicle type, route, frequency, ADA accessibility
  • Entity & insurance — who is the operating entity; nonprofit vs. commercial structure; event insurance and liquor licensing for the beer garden
