## John Jeremy Norman — Fri, Jun 12, 2026

Hi Lindsay - I can definitely relate to the multiple/simultaneous calls on your attention. As I have considered the layout for this event, I have come to some conclusions. Essentially, the following thoughts are an expansion of the central Holiday themed village with our existing buildings - all of which create a Holiday themed "German" village within the village of Marlborough, NH. Please consider the following list of my current considerations.

- We should use the Municipal parking lot as the main area for vendor set up. Your usual setup with multiple tents/vendors.
- The municipal lot can be organized to host a number of vendors - I would say at least 75 in your usual format. We can measure it off and allocate, make a floor/ground plan reflective of appropriate spacing.
- Perhaps the areas of/around the Community House and along the back piece of property along the lane which joins the municipal lot and the Marlborough House.
- The Main Hall/Church would be a Hofbräu House of sorts with a winter "beer garden" and a holiday selection of beverages and themed food offerings.
- The Main House will be Christmas House fantasy with Arts and Crafts - maybe ornament makers, Christmas through and through - per room as we discussed or other.
- Stark Level Solutions would be HQ, check in for vendors, pre-set up, green room for performers/workers, production, and bathrooms - which will need to be discussed... we have bathrooms at all our locations but with the size of this event, a set of restroom trailers would not be a bad idea and maybe necessary. If we can avoid port-a-potties, that would be again optimal.
- The tent vendors would be organized in the municipal lot - attractively lit with themed lights, Holiday signs etc. pointing in various directions.

As I have continued to consider the layout for this event, there are several large parking lots within walking distance of this location:

- The local public school has an enormous lot and is about 1/2 a mile from the center of town.
- There is a large area for parking at the end we visited by McNabb Slabs.
- There is also a large area for parking directly behind the former location of Mother's where our studio is now.
- Also in walking distance is another large lot attached to a general store across from our Town Hall and police HQ.

Mind you, the discussion of setup, logistics, parking, and overall format of the total event in this manner will require a full discussion with the town and the local police. While these parking areas will be within a short walk of the "central event," we will need to include stretch golf carts "Christmas Cabooses" or other thematically decorated conveyance for visitors to and from the central happening in the middle of town.

This type of flow will really play up the overall feeling of a Christmas village inside a village. Our biggest issue will be the management of traffic and people along 101. There will HAVE to be designated crossing points where traffic guards will be stationed along with other appointees that will manage the flow of people. Optimally, we will set up a perimeter barrier with entrances to the festivities that correspond with existing driveways etc.

A perimeter "prop fence" in keeping with the theme could tie to or with our neighbors including the GETTY, the fire department, and assisted living homes which form one side of the "Christmas village". Those residents will of course be offered free VIP passes for all festivities which should be an easy sell for this security aspect. Our properties along with Main Crust form the other perimeters... We are very friendly with our neighbors and Main Crust - so the other heavy lifting would be with the town and gaining the necessary permissions.

THIS ENCLOSED TEMPLATE IS THE PERFECT PLUG IN FORMAT YOU ARE SEARCHING FOR AND WILL WORK FOR ANY SITUATION FROM A SUMMER MUSIC FESTIVAL, THEATRICAL EVENT, FALL HALLOWEEN FLING, EASTER BUNNY HOP, OR COMMUNITY ACTION POLITICAL EVENT!!!! IT WORKS FOR ANYTHING!!!!!

This framework is $$$$$$$ in terms of so many different considerations including plug and play applications, an eye/attention catching event for the town of Marlborough and Keene and the Monadnock Region in general.

This scenario could definitely accommodate thousands of individuals.

I have mentioned this to some of the town businesses and they ARE ALL for it!! I would imagine 5 security staff would need to be hired. Additionally, 5-6 volunteers or low paid staff to act as runners/junior producers. You will need 2 people at each ticket booth entrance. They could be equipped with iPad and Square devices or other to ring people in, check tickets etc. I would say another 4 people to run the Cabooses depending on what the final setup is, number of anticipated guests, etc.

IF your exhibitor price is $250 for a booth, that's $22,500 right there with 100 participants. If we do larger or higher end options like in the house or just larger... so on and so forth... If we want to get 5000 per weekend at $5/ticket/family... that's another $25k right there... just thinking about stuff off the top of my head...

YEAH!!! This will work... town approval - with your documentation of success and proven track record will be no problem. I will also get ahead of the town by getting the support of key individuals here and then, essentially, telling the town what we are gonna do! Looking forward to your thoughts...

-john jeremy
